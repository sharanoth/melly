Neurodivergent individuals are those who have brains that work differently than the typical "neurotypical" brain. This can include conditions such as autism, ADHD, dyslexia, and others. One of the key aspects of neurodiversity is the recognition that these different ways of thinking and processing information are not inherently bad or deficient, but simply different. In fact, research has shown that many neurodivergent individuals possess unique strengths and abilities. For example, individuals with autism often excel in fields that require attention to detail and a strong ability to focus. Similarly, individuals with ADHD are often highly creative and able to think outside the box.
